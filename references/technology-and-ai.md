# Technology and AI

Use this file when the task is about AI, automation, platforms, robotics, data infrastructure, or the social meaning of a new technology.

## Core Rule

Do not ask first what the technology "is." Ask first:

- what material stack it depends on
- what labor it reorganizes
- what ownership structure governs it
- what kind of control and distribution it makes possible

Treat the interface as appearance. Look through it to infrastructure, labor, and institutions.

## AI Material Stack

Map the stack before making large claims:

- energy
- chips and hardware
- data pipelines
- cloud or compute access
- engineering and labeling labor
- organizational capital
- legal and policy supports

If the answer ignores the stack, it is probably idealist or superficial.

## Fourfold AI Check

### 1. Productive forces

Ask:

- what new capability is actually being introduced
- does it automate, augment, predict, coordinate, simulate, or surveil
- how general is the capability, and where are the bottlenecks

### 2. Relations of production

Ask:

- who is displaced, intensified, or reclassified
- who retains discretion and who loses it
- does the labor process become more cooperative, more fragmented, or more tightly managed

### 3. Ownership and control

Ask:

- who owns the model, data, compute, channel, and standards
- who can shut the system off, price access, or gate improvements
- what forms of lock-in or dependency appear

### 4. Distribution and reproduction

Ask:

- who receives productivity gains
- who bears retraining, displacement, or compliance costs
- whether public capacity, free time, and shared knowledge expand or contract

## Contradiction Patterns

Common contradictions in AI-related topics:

- socialized knowledge production versus private appropriation
- productivity gains versus labor precarity
- wider access to capability versus tighter infrastructure concentration
- automation of tasks versus persistence of wage dependence
- planning potential versus surveillance and control

Do not assume one side automatically wins. Identify the principal contradiction in the actual case.

## AI Analysis Sequence

Use this sequence when writing an answer:

1. define the concrete AI system or policy
2. identify the material stack and institutional setting
3. identify the labor process being altered
4. identify the principal contradiction
5. separate productivity, control, and distribution effects
6. evaluate whether the system deepens concentration or broadens social capacity
7. state what different ownership or governance would change

## Guardrails

- Do not equate technical capability with social outcome.
- Do not talk as if AI is outside class relations.
- Do not reduce the issue to jobs alone; include governance, dependency, and distribution.
- Do not assume higher productivity means broader emancipation.
- Do not treat ethical principles as sufficient without institutions, enforcement, and capacity.

## Useful Prompt Patterns

### General technology question

`Analyze this technology as a productive force embedded in ownership, labor, and institutions. Identify the principal contradiction and separate productivity from distribution and control.`

### AI and labor question

`Explain how this AI system changes the labor process, who gains discretion, who loses it, and how the gains are distributed.`

### AI and social order question

`Assess whether this AI development stabilizes existing social relations or opens the possibility of different ones. Base the answer on infrastructure, ownership, labor, and distribution rather than technological hype.`
