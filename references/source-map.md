# Source Map

This file records the online materials used to shape the workflow in this skill. Use it when refining the skill or when you need to justify why a step exists.

## Source-to-Method Mapping

### Karl Marx (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)

Link: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/marx/

Operational takeaway:

- start from material production, social relations, and productive power when explaining social form
- treat ideology and institutions as historically conditioned, not self-grounding
- put the object into motion rather than treating the present arrangement as eternal

### Hegel's Dialectics (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)

Link: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/hegel-dialectics/

Operational takeaway:

- analyze objects as relations in movement
- look for internal contradiction rather than isolated features
- treat development as structured transformation, not simple linear change

### Historical Materialism (Britannica)

Link: https://www.britannica.com/topic/historical-materialism

Operational takeaway:

- explain ideas and institutions in relation to material and historical development
- prioritize historical emergence and structural conditions over moralized snapshots

### Dialectical Materialism (Britannica)

Link: https://www.britannica.com/topic/dialectical-materialism

Operational takeaway:

- treat reality as processual and contradictory
- use quantity-to-quality shifts and interconnected development when the case involves thresholds or ruptures

### Idealism (Britannica)

Link: https://www.britannica.com/topic/idealism

Operational takeaway:

- use idealism as a comparison frame when a claim explains reality mainly by ideas, values, or consciousness
- ask what such explanations miss about material constraints and social interests

### Materialism (Britannica)

Link: https://www.britannica.com/topic/materialism-philosophy

Operational takeaway:

- keep explanation grounded in matter, conditions, and practical life
- resist replacing mechanism with rhetoric

### Theses on Feuerbach (Marx)

Link: https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1845/theses/theses.htm

Operational takeaway:

- do not treat thought as detached contemplation
- connect knowledge to sensuous human activity and practice
- include a practical test when the task involves judgment or recommendation

### A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy, Preface (Marx)

Link: https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1859/critique-pol-economy/preface.htm

Operational takeaway:

- use base-superstructure analysis when law, politics, morality, or culture are central
- avoid crude determinism by explaining mediation and historical sequence

### On Practice (Mao Zedong)

Link: https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-1/mswv1_16.htm

Operational takeaway:

- move from concrete experience to conceptual judgment and back to practice
- do not stop at verbal correctness; ask what reality would confirm the answer

### On Contradiction (Mao Zedong)

Link: https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/mao/selected-works/volume-1/mswv1_17.htm

Operational takeaway:

- identify the principal contradiction instead of flattening all issues into a list
- distinguish principal from secondary aspects
- adapt the method to the specific contradiction of the case rather than applying formulas mechanically

### Grundrisse, Introduction (Marx)

Link: https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1857/grundrisse/ch01.htm

Operational takeaway:

- move from the concrete case to abstraction and then return to the concrete whole
- use abstraction as a tool, not as a substitute for the real case

### Generative AI and jobs: A 2025 update (ILO)

Link: https://www.ilo.org/publications/generative-ai-and-jobs-2025-update

Operational takeaway:

- analyze AI in terms of transformation of work, not only replacement
- treat labor-process change and social dialogue as central variables, not afterthoughts
- ask how gains in productivity and working conditions are mediated institutionally

### AI and the Global Productivity Divide (OECD, 2025)

Link: https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2025/12/ai-and-the-global-productivity-divide_f47026c5/c315ea90-en.pdf

Operational takeaway:

- separate technical potential from actual adoption and from uneven cross-country outcomes
- include complementary factors such as skills, infrastructure, and industrial structure
- do not universalize frontier-economy AI effects to every context

### Artificial intelligence, its diffusion and uses in manufacturing (OECD, 2021)

Link: https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2021/07/artificial-intelligence-its-diffusion-and-uses-in-manufacturing_2696a54f/249e2003-en.pdf

Operational takeaway:

- treat AI as embedded across the production process, from design to fabrication to logistics
- ask about diffusion barriers, not just peak capability
- include evaluation capacity and policy design when discussing deployment

### UNESCO AI Literacy Training for Civil Servants / Ethics of AI implementation

Link: https://www.unesco.org/ethics-ai/en/articles/unescos-ai-literacy-training-civil-servants-empowering-ethical-ai-governance-around-world-0

Operational takeaway:

- include governance capacity, procurement, accountability, data governance, and public consultation
- do not reduce ethics to values language; connect it to institutions, due diligence, and implementation
- treat the state as an active mediator of AI outcomes rather than a passive observer

## How to Use This Map

When extending the skill:

- use SEP and Britannica for compact conceptual clarification
- use Marx and Mao texts for the operational moves behind the method
- add new references only if they change the workflow, not merely because they repeat the same concepts
