# Frameworks

Use this file when the task needs a denser conceptual toolkit than the core workflow in `SKILL.md`.

## Core Concepts

### Materialism

Prioritize material life, practice, production, institutions, and social relations when explaining consciousness, culture, and ideas.

Working questions:

- What material conditions make this belief, institution, or behavior possible?
- What practical interests does it organize or protect?
- What social relation does it express?
- What practice would test whether this explanation is real or merely verbal?

### Idealism

Treat ideas, values, consciousness, will, spirit, or discourse as primary explanatory drivers.

Use it as a contrast class:

- What does an idealist reading explain well?
- What does it leave unexplained about material constraint, class interest, or institutional reproduction?
- Is it mistaking moral language or stated intention for the actual mechanism?

### Metaphysical Thinking

Treat objects as static, isolated, fixed in essence, or merely externally related.

Diagnostic questions:

- Is the argument treating a historically produced structure as eternal?
- Is it isolating one factor from the total relation?
- Is it ignoring internal contradiction and development?
- Is it describing a snapshot while missing process, thresholds, and transformation?

### Dialectical Thinking

Treat objects as connected, dynamic, internally contradictory, and historically changing.

Diagnostic questions:

- What internal contradiction drives development here?
- What appears stable but is actually conditional?
- What transformation becomes possible once quantity crosses into quality?
- What relation is primary, and what relations are derivative or mediated through it?

### Contradiction Analysis

Use contradiction analysis to identify the engine of movement.

Check:

- main contradiction
- secondary contradictions
- principal aspect
- non-principal aspect
- antagonistic or non-antagonistic form
- conditions under which aspects can transform into one another
- practical consequence of misidentifying the principal contradiction

### Historical Materialism

Explain social forms historically through productive forces, relations of production, class structure, and institutional reproduction.

Check:

- what stage or transition is this part of
- what changed materially before the ideological or political shift
- what class interests align with the current arrangement
- what contradictions accumulate over time
- what this case looked like before it took its current form

### Fourfold Structure Check

Use this check when the object is an economy, sector, institution, platform, or technology.

Map four linked layers:

- productive forces: tools, compute, infrastructure, skills, science, workflows
- relations of production: who works, who directs, who depends on whom, under what rules
- ownership and control: who owns assets, IP, platforms, data, networks, or fixed capital
- distribution and reproduction: who captures gains, who absorbs losses, how daily life is reproduced

Diagnostic questions:

- which layer is changing fastest
- which older relation is now constraining a newer productive force
- who gains productivity and who loses autonomy
- what part of the system is socialized in practice but privately appropriated in result

### Base and Superstructure

Use this when the topic involves ideology, law, morality, education, media, or politics.

Map:

- base: property, production, labor, exchange, distribution
- superstructure: state, law, culture, ideology, moral language

Then ask:

- how does the superstructure stabilize or reorganize the base
- where does the superstructure feed back into production or social control
- what part is flexible, and what part is structurally constrained
- what would change in law, discourse, or culture if the underlying material relation changed

### From the Particular to the General

Use a disciplined movement:

1. observe the concrete case
2. identify recurring relation or mechanism
3. formulate a general claim
4. return to the concrete case for verification

Do not treat anecdote as law. Do not treat law-like tendency as a detached slogan.

### Practice and Knowledge

Use a practice-theory-practice loop:

1. gather perceptual or concrete material
2. form a rational or abstract judgment
3. return to practice for verification

Diagnostic questions:

- What evidence or practical outcome would validate this claim?
- What new contradiction becomes visible once the recommendation is applied?
- Is this answer actionable, or only verbally satisfying?

### Technology and Mediation

Use when the topic involves AI, automation, digital platforms, or infrastructure.

Diagnostic questions:

- what material stack makes the technology possible: chips, data, energy, cloud, logistics, labor
- what social relation is hidden behind the interface
- does the technology centralize control, diffuse capability, or both at once
- is the main effect labor substitution, labor intensification, coordination, surveillance, or enclosure
- what institutions mediate the outcome: property law, procurement, platform rules, regulation, unions, public provision

## Additional Dialectical Categories

Use only when they improve clarity.

### Essence and Appearance

- appearance: what is immediately visible
- essence: the underlying relation or mechanism

Ask what visible surface conceals or refracts.

### Quantity and Quality

Use when gradual accumulation suddenly produces a break, threshold, or phase shift.

Examples:

- cost pressure becomes institutional crisis
- small policy changes become a legitimacy problem
- incremental technical change reorganizes labor relations

### Universal and Particular

Use to avoid two bad moves:

- abstract universalism with no real case
- raw empiricism with no explanatory pattern

Working move:

- derive a general mechanism from repeated features of the case
- then test whether the mechanism illuminates rather than erases the case

### Content and Form

Use when the same underlying relation appears in different legal, cultural, or rhetorical forms.

### Concrete and Abstract

Use when the task risks becoming over-theoretical or over-descriptive.

- abstract: a category, mechanism, or simplified relation
- concrete: a structured whole with many determinations

Working rule:

- abstract to clarify
- return to the concrete to avoid emptiness

## Recommended Writing Moves

### For essays or long answers

Use this spine:

1. define the object
2. describe concrete conditions
3. identify the contradiction
4. explain historical formation
5. compare materialist and idealist readings if useful
6. rise to the general claim
7. return to the case and test the claim
8. close with practical or theoretical implications

### For short answers

Compress to:

- material basis
- contradiction
- historical movement
- conclusion

### For critique

Ask:

- what is being naturalized
- what is being depoliticized
- what social interest benefits from this framing
- what contradiction is being concealed

### For diagnosis

Ask:

- what is the principal contradiction right now
- what is the principal aspect of that contradiction
- what are the secondary contradictions that can wait
- what intervention would change the structure rather than the surface

### For strategy

Ask:

- what material resources are actually available
- what superstructural obstacles stabilize the current arrangement
- what threshold or phase shift matters most
- what sequence of actions matches the real balance of forces

### For technology analysis

Ask:

- what productive force is actually new here
- what relation of production is being reinforced or destabilized
- who owns the infrastructure and the data exhaust
- how productivity gains are divided between capital, labor, and the public
- whether the technology widens social capacity or deepens dependency

## Trigger-Like User Requests

This skill is a strong fit for prompts such as:

- analyze this social issue from a dialectical materialist perspective
- compare materialism and idealism in this debate
- explain this policy with contradiction analysis
- use historical materialism to interpret this event
- critique this cultural phenomenon through base and superstructure
- move from the concrete case to the general mechanism
