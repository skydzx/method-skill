# Method Errors

Use this file when an answer feels empty, moralized, static, overly personal, or detached from reality.

## Core Rule

Most weak answers fail not because they lack concepts, but because they violate method.

The fastest repair loop is:

1. identify the failure type
2. restate the object concretely
3. recover material conditions
4. locate the principal contradiction
5. return to practice or implication

## Common Errors

### 1. Moralizing instead of explaining

Symptom:

- the draft mainly says something is bad, unjust, selfish, backward, or hypocritical

Why it fails:

- attitude replaces mechanism
- condemnation appears before causality

Repair:

- ask what conditions reproduce the behavior
- ask whose interests the arrangement serves
- ask what contradiction gives the behavior its stability

### 2. Psychologizing a structural issue

Symptom:

- the draft explains outcomes mainly through greed, laziness, fear, ignorance, or character

Why it fails:

- personal motive is used where role, incentive, institution, or dependency matters more

Repair:

- replace motives with positions, incentives, and constraints
- ask what different people would do if placed in the same structure

### 3. Individualizing what is institutionally reproduced

Symptom:

- the answer treats a repeated social pattern as if it were a series of isolated personal choices

Why it fails:

- it misses how law, market pressure, organizational rules, or ideology reproduce the pattern

Repair:

- ask what mechanism reproduces the same result across many actors

### 4. Flattening all contradictions

Symptom:

- the answer lists many problems but does not rank them

Why it fails:

- without a principal contradiction, no line of explanation or action is possible

Repair:

- ask which contradiction currently structures the others
- ask which one, if changed, would shift the whole situation most

### 5. Freezing a moving process

Symptom:

- the answer treats the current form as natural, fixed, or timeless

Why it fails:

- history disappears, so transformation also disappears

Repair:

- ask how this form arose
- ask what prior stage looked different
- ask what current pressures may change it

### 6. Technological determinism

Symptom:

- the answer treats technology as an independent force that automatically decides the social outcome

Why it fails:

- it ignores ownership, labor process, institutions, and distribution

Repair:

- ask who owns the infrastructure
- ask how the technology is deployed
- ask who captures the gains and who bears the costs

### 7. Mechanical economic determinism

Symptom:

- the answer uses "the economy determines everything" as a shortcut

Why it fails:

- it erases mediation through law, politics, culture, and institutions

Repair:

- explain the path from material relation to institutional effect
- ask how the superstructure stabilizes, refracts, or modifies the base

### 8. Abstracting away from the case

Symptom:

- the answer sounds theoretically correct but could apply to anything

Why it fails:

- abstraction never returns to the concrete object

Repair:

- add one concrete condition, one actual mechanism, and one practical implication

### 9. Confusing symptom with cause

Symptom:

- the answer treats visible crisis effects as the root problem

Why it fails:

- it stays at the level of appearance

Repair:

- ask what produces the repeated symptom
- ask what hidden relation or bottleneck generates it

### 10. Ending without practice

Symptom:

- the answer stops after analysis and never states what follows

Why it fails:

- method is not completed; judgment is not tested

Repair:

- state what can actually change
- state what evidence or action would confirm the analysis

## Quick Diagnostic Questions

Use these when revising:

- Am I explaining a structure or merely judging it?
- Am I giving motives where I should give conditions?
- Have I identified a principal contradiction?
- Have I put the issue into history?
- Have I returned from abstraction to the concrete case?
- Have I stated what this analysis implies in practice?
